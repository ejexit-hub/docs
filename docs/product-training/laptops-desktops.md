# Laptops & Desktops

## Introduction
This manual helps personnel at Exit Technologies Inc. understand laptops and desktops, their internal components, and the opportunities for recovering valuable components for resale.

## 1. Overview
Laptops and desktops are general-purpose computing devices used widely across personal, educational, and business environments.

### Types of Laptops & Desktops

<Accordion title="Business Laptops" description="Professional laptops optimized for reliability" type="info">
  <p><strong>Overview:</strong> Built for durability, security, and productivity in professional environments.</p>
  <p><strong>Common Valuable Components:</strong> CPUs, RAM, SSDs, LCD Screens, Batteries.</p>
  <p><strong>Examples:</strong> Dell Latitude, HP EliteBook, Lenovo ThinkPad.</p>
  <p><strong>Best For:</strong> Enterprise users, remote professionals, frequent travelers.</p>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Consumer Laptops" description="General-use laptops for personal applications" type="tip">
  <p><strong>Overview:</strong> Everyday-use laptops designed for home users, students, and casual users.</p>
  <p><strong>Common Valuable Components:</strong> CPUs, RAM, SSDs/HDDs, LCD Screens.</p>
  <p><strong>Examples:</strong> Dell Inspiron, HP Pavilion, Apple MacBook Air.</p>
  <p><strong>Best For:</strong> General computing, personal use, education.</p>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Gaming Desktops & Laptops" description="High-performance devices for intensive tasks" type="note">
  <p><strong>Overview:</strong> High-powered systems optimized for gaming and performance-intensive applications.</p>
  <p><strong>Common Valuable Components:</strong> Dedicated GPUs, CPUs, High-Speed RAM, SSDs, Motherboards.</p>
  <p><strong>Examples:</strong> Alienware laptops/desktops, ASUS ROG, MSI gaming series.</p>
  <p><strong>Best For:</strong> Gaming enthusiasts, video editing, content creation.</p>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Workstations & Professional Desktops" description="Powerful desktop systems for demanding professional tasks" type="info">
  <p><strong>Overview:</strong> Desktops designed for resource-intensive workloads, CAD applications, and video editing.</p>
  <p><strong>Common Valuable Components:</strong> CPUs, RAM, GPUs, SSDs, Motherboards, Power Supplies.</p>
  <p><strong>Examples:</strong> Dell Precision, HP Z-series, Lenovo ThinkStation.</p>
  <p><strong>Best For:</strong> Engineering, architecture, professional multimedia editing.</p>
</Accordion>

## 2. Valuable Internal Components
Knowing which components to recover is crucial for maximizing returns.

<Accordion title="Processors (CPUs)" description="Core computing units" type="info">
  <p><strong>Description:</strong> Intel Core i-series (i5, i7, i9) and AMD Ryzen processors are typically valuable.</p>
  <p><strong>Identification:</strong> Model, generation, core count, clock speed.</p>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Memory (RAM)" description="High-demand memory modules" type="info">
  <p><strong>Description:</strong> DDR4 and DDR5 RAM modules have substantial resale value, especially higher-capacity modules.</p>
  <p><strong>Identification:</strong> Capacity (GB), type (DDR4, DDR5), speed.</p>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Storage Drives (SSDs/HDDs)" description="Reliable storage components" type="info">
  <p><strong>Description:</strong> SSDs (especially NVMe) have high market value; HDDs maintain moderate resale value.</p>
  <p><strong>Identification:</strong> Capacity, interface (NVMe, SATA), health condition.</p>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="LCD Displays (Laptops)" description="Laptop screens with good resale value" type="info">
  <p><strong>Description:</strong> High-resolution and touch-sensitive LCD screens can command significant resale prices.</p>
  <p><strong>Identification:</strong> Screen size, resolution, touch capability, panel type (IPS, OLED).</p>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Graphics Cards (GPUs, Gaming Systems)" description="Dedicated graphics units for desktops" type="info">
  <p><strong>Description:</strong> GPUs from gaming and workstation desktops retain significant value.</p>
  <p><strong>Identification:</strong> Model, memory capacity, generation.</p>
</Accordion>

## 3. Key Specifications for Laptop & Desktop Assessment

### CPU Specifications
- Generation and Core Count
- Clock Speed and Socket Type

### RAM Specifications
- Capacity (GB)
- Type (DDR3, DDR4, DDR5)
- Speed (MHz)

### Storage Specifications
- Drive type (SSD/HDD/NVMe)
- Capacity and health rating
- Interface (SATA, NVMe)

### GPU Specifications (if applicable)
- Model and Generation
- Dedicated memory (VRAM)
- Condition and usability

### Display Specifications (Laptop)
- Screen Size and Resolution
- Panel Type (IPS, OLED)
- Touch Capability

## 4. Common Use Cases

### Business & Enterprise
Reliable, secure computing for professional environments and remote work.

### Education & Personal Use
Affordable, versatile systems for students and personal computing tasks.

### Gaming & Multimedia
Powerful systems optimized for immersive gaming and high-performance multimedia editing.

### Professional Workstations
Advanced desktops for CAD, rendering, scientific research, and engineering tasks.

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions
- **"How do I know which laptop or desktop parts are most valuable?"**
  - Typically, CPUs, RAM, SSDs, GPUs, and high-resolution laptop screens yield the best returns.

- **"Do older laptops and desktops still have resale value?"**
  - Yes, especially if components like CPUs, RAM, and storage drives are in good condition.

## 6. Practical Tips
- Document specifications clearly during disassembly to simplify valuation.
- Protect screens, connectors, and delicate parts during handling to maintain resale value.
